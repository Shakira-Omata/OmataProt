Develop a new web application called **SalamaHub**.

SalamaHub is a youth-friendly digital platform that provides accessible, accurate, inclusive, and judgment-free sexual and reproductive health and rights (SRHR) information for young people in Kenya. The platform should serve all youth, while intentionally including young people with disabilities and other marginalized or underserved groups.

At this stage, it is a prototype, so data can be served from local JSON files.

The application should have the following goals:
- Provide safe and easy access to SRHR information
- Promote inclusive learning for all young people
- Offer rights-based and affirming educational content
- Help users privately find support and trusted services
- Work well in low-bandwidth environments
- Be mobile-first, responsive, and highly accessible

The application should have the following main sections:
- Home
- Learn About SRHR
- Know Your Rights
- Diverse Corner
- Questions & Myths
- Get Support
- Guides & Downloads
- About This Platform

Some of the pages required are:
- Login page
- Sign up page
- Home / Dashboard page
- Learn About SRHR listing page
- SRHR topic detail page
- Know Your Rights page
- Diverse Corner page
- Questions & Myths page
- Get Support page
- Guides & Downloads page
- About This Platform page
- Accessibility preferences panel or settings section

The Home / Dashboard page should include:
- an image that is inclusive that is a group of teenagers sitted on a round table discussing about SRHR and they are all different races and ethnicities and also include a person with a disability
- A welcoming hero section with a headline such as: “SRHR Education for All”
- A short supportive description
- Strong call-to-action buttons such as “Start Learning” and “Find Support”
- A “Test Your Knowledge” quiz card or section
- Featured topic cards or learning entry points
- A visible “Inclusive Access” or “Accessibility Support” card
- Quick links to major sections
- A calm, spacious layout that feels safe and easy to navigate

Under Learn About SRHR, include key topics such as:
- Overview of SRHR
- Menstrual Health
- Family Planning
- STI/HIV Prevention
- Pregnancy and Childbirth
- Abortion and Safe Choices
- Hygiene and Body Care
- Bodily Autonomy
- Adolescent Health
- Maternal Health

The Know Your Rights section should include:
- Bodily autonomy and consent
- Privacy and confidentiality in healthcare
- Access to youth-friendly health services
- Freedom from violence, stigma, and discrimination
- Rights of young people seeking SRHR information and care
- Kenya-relevant and rights-based educational content

The Diverse Corner section should be a clearly visible core part of the platform, not an afterthought. It should include:
- SRHR for young people with disabilities
- Disability and sexuality myths
- Consent and bodily autonomy
- Inclusive menstrual health education
- Accessible STI and reproductive health information
- Affirming content for diverse and underserved youth
- Content that reflects real-life inclusion and dignity

The Questions & Myths section should include:
- Common SRHR myths and facts
- Anonymous-feeling educational content
- Simple, clear answers written in supportive language
- Easy-to-scan cards or accordion sections

The Get Support section should include:
- Youth-friendly SRHR resources
- Mental health support resources
- Referral pathways or support directories
- Private and inclusive support options
- Support considerations for young people with disabilities and marginalized users
- Emergency or urgent help guidance where relevant

The Guides & Downloads section should include:
- Downloadable guides and educational resources
- Simple explainers
- Youth-friendly toolkits
- Posters, PDFs, or printable support materials
- Accessible resource labels such as audio-ready, easy-read, or screen-reader friendly

The About This Platform page should include:
- What SalamaHub is
- Why it exists
- Its inclusion and accessibility values
- A message of safety, dignity, and empowerment
- A short note that the platform is for education and support, not a replacement for professional care

Strong accessibility and usability features must be built across the entire application, including:
- Adjustable text size
- High contrast mode
- Keyboard-friendly navigation
- Screen-reader friendly semantic structure
- Large touch-friendly buttons
- Clear spacing and strong visual hierarchy
- Simple language / easy-read option
- Audio or read-aloud support
- Caption-ready multimedia support
- Low-bandwidth-friendly performance
- Responsive layouts for mobile, tablet, and desktop

Frontend design:
- The application should be mobile-first but fully responsive
- Use React for the application
- Use Tailwind CSS for styling
- Create reusable components and a clean project structure
- Use lucide-react icons or equivalent for clean iconography
- Use local JSON mock data for prototype content
- The interface should feel warm, calm, inclusive, modern, and community-centered
- Avoid making it look too clinical, too corporate, or too crowded

Layout and UI direction:
- Use a top navigation bar with the SalamaHub logo and key navigation links
- Include a sidebar or structured side navigation for desktop
- The dashboard inspiration should match a clean educational layout with:
  - a simple top nav
  - a left sidebar with icons and labels
  - a main content area with cards and sections
  - soft blue, grey, and white tones
  - rounded buttons and cards
  - clear spacing and readable typography
- The visual style should balance trust, warmth, and clarity
- The design should feel like a safe digital learning and support space

Authentication pages:
- Login and Sign up screens should feel welcoming and simple
- Include email and password fields
- Include Google sign-in option
- Include forgot password link on login
- Include a sign-up link from login and a login link from sign-up
- Use a friendly visual presentation, but ensure readability and accessibility remain strong
